# Internship Journal

## Pre‑Internship Observation – Internal Career Architecture

**Date:** July 2026

---

### Observation

While reviewing current Equinix internal job postings, I noticed two distinct patterns.

The first is that Toronto hosts a significant number of senior‑level positions across multiple business units, not only data centre operations. In addition to Critical Facilities and Customer Operations, Toronto supports Operations Engineering, Design & Construction, Technology, Finance, AI, Product Management, and Corporate Leadership.

This reinforced that Equinix is much larger than a collection of data centres — it is a global technology company with many internal career paths.

---

### Career Progression

Several Operations Engineering positions particularly stood out:

- Senior Mechanical Engineer  
- Senior Staff Electrical Engineer  
- Staff Controls Engineer  

These roles generally required approximately **5–10 years of relevant experience** along with significant technical qualifications.

The advertised compensation ranged from roughly **$80 000 to over $240 000 annually**, illustrating the long‑term earning potential available after developing expertise.

Rather than viewing these roles as immediate goals, I see them as examples of where sustained technical development can eventually lead.

---

### Leadership Observation

I also reviewed the background of the Brampton IBX Manager.

His career progression demonstrates that advancement was built over decades of experience:

- Electronics Engineering Technology diploma (Humber College)  
- Control Systems Technologist  
- Computer Hardware Specialist  
- Facilities Manager (Q9 Networks)  
- Regional Data Centre Manager (Bell)  
- IBX Manager (Equinix)  

An important insight is that he was not originally hired directly into Equinix.

His Facilities Manager position at Q9 Networks transitioned into Equinix following the company’s acquisition, illustrating that industry consolidation can create unexpected career opportunities.

---

### Educational Insight

One observation that surprised me was that experienced Equinix leaders reached senior management without completing university degrees.

Instead, their careers were built on:

- technical college education  
- decades of practical experience  
- progressive responsibility  
- industry knowledge  
- leadership development  

This reinforces that long‑term experience and consistent performance can be as important as formal academic credentials for advancement in this field.

---

### Personal Reflection

Prior to this review, my focus was simply on obtaining an internship.

After examining the broader organization, I now better understand that the internship represents entry into a much larger professional ecosystem.

Rather than focusing only on my first position, I intend to use the internship to:

- understand how Critical Facilities and Customer Operations interact  
- observe experienced technicians, engineers, and managers  
- identify which technical disciplines align best with my interests  
- build the experience required for future opportunities  

This reinforces a key principle within my knowledge system:

**Use the internship to gather evidence before deciding on a long‑term specialization.**

The goal is not to rush toward a senior title, but to develop the technical foundation and professional reputation that make those opportunities possible over time.
